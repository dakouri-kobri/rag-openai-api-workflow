# Hyperskill Manifesto

Learning to program is not only about knowing specific tools and concepts. It is also about acquiring mental skills that help you solve complex problems.

These skills develop best in a setting that resembles real software development.

## The skills and abilities you’ll develop

- Applying concepts and tools of a programming language and basic computer science
- Decomposing complex problems
- Searching for the information needed to solve a problem
- Overcoming frustration when your solution doesn’t work and debugging efficiently
- Using professional tools for development and becoming familiar with complex project development processes
- Creating and updating your own portfolio

## The learning principles Hyperskill builds on

### Active learning
Skills are developed by trying out and applying concepts right away to real-life problems. Each theoretical piece is followed by practical assignments. Passive consumption (reading/watching without application) may be enjoyable, but it is ineffective.

### Knowledge transfer
The goal is the ability to apply what you learn in new contexts. This is why the platform emphasizes tools and processes used in actual development.

### Extensive practice
You solve multiple similar problems per topic until the concept becomes automatic. This reduces cognitive load later, so you can focus on applying knowledge in your project rather than recalling theory.

### Project-based learning
Learning is driven by a concrete challenge posed by a project. Projects are complex by nature, so they are broken into stages and decomposed to guide you through building a working solution.

### Social learning
A community of active learners helps people succeed. Collaborating and solving problems with others is a career-relevant skill.

### Driven by practical goals
Projects range from introductory programs to full applications. Motivation is strongest when you build something real that matters to you, so you can eventually choose what to build based on your interests (backend, apps, etc.).

## Learn while gradually growing your project

- Each stage of a project poses a challenge.
- Topics in your curriculum provide the knowledge needed to meet that challenge.
- Each stage grows in complexity, teaching you to reuse and expand previous code.
- This approach helps you experience “reverse decomposition” and learn how to break down problems in the future.

## Knowledge map (concept)

A multi-purpose knowledge base that supports offering multiple projects and building a unique curriculum for each. You can use it to see an overview of what you need to master, but the recommended path is to learn by building a project.

## Study plan (concept)

A personal plan designed for effective learning. Study plans are created as sequences of topics aligned to project stages, so you learn while building a working application.
